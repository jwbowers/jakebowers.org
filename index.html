Jake Bowers

Jake Bowers is a professor in the Departments of Political Science and Statistics at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. He serves as the Methods Director for the Evidence in Governance and Politics (EGAP) network and co-founded the Causal Inference for Social Impact Lab. His research focuses on the design and analysis of randomized experiments and observational studies to understand political behavior and public policy.

From 2015 to 2020 and starting again in 2022, he has worked with the Office of Evaluation Sciences in the General Services Administration of the US Federal Government. During 2015 the OES was publicly known as the White House Social and Behavioral Sciences Team. From 2019 to 2021, he was a Senior Scientist at The Policy Lab working on coordinated RCTs focused on early childhood language learning, COVID-related projects, and processes for reproducible data analysis for teams using multiple analysis languages. He worked on projects at the intersection of the social and behavioral sciences and public policy as a Research Affiliate and Fellow at the Center for Advanced Study in the Social and Behavioral Sciences, including the Causal Inference for Social Impact Lab. From 2018 to 2022, he was the Methods Director of the Evidence in Governance and Politics (EGAP) network. Some of his work with EGAP led to an online textbook on field experiments: The Theory and Practice of Field Experiments: An Introduction from the EGAP Learning Days, with translations into Spanish and French. He helped to start Research4Impact, an organization that connects researchers and practitioners to improve public policy, governance, and advocacy. You can see one measure of the impact of his work on his Google Scholar profile or on Impact Story.
